Game Summary

Moonsighted is a third-person hack-and-slash adventure about cultural and political strife in the face of technology and progress amongst moon-worshiping moths trapped in a cavern. Play as Mairu, a miner that harnesses the power of cave crystals known as ilarka. Explore dangerous depths to fight off cavernous critters to acquire more ilarka and help lead your people back to moonlight.

Contributions

- Implemented modular wave-based enemy combat system using scriptable objects.
- Developed enemy AI using Unity NavMesh with player tracking and different attacks.
- Created systems for destroying crystal cores and collecting crystals.
- Modeled a 3D graybox of the village cave area as well as the final village environmental assets.
- Designed variations of moth wings for moonsighted and regular moths.
- Concepted character designs as well as various in-game props.
- Collaborated with the team to assemble the level in Unity, and added set dressing with asset prefabs.
- Conducted playtests to gather feedback about the game builds.

Working on Sliptime Sleuth gave me the opportunity to practice low-poly character and interior modeling. I also learned how to handle and organize a large set of assets so that art was completed by the deadlines. I worked with the designers to block out layouts for each scene, and then compiled the individual assets from other artists along with myself to create the final level formats.

Sliptime Sleuth also gave me the opportunity to work collaboratively as a programmer on the game. This taught me source control and how to split scripting work so that teammates were not overriding each other's progress.
